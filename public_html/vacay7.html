Great Migration Classic

Day 1: Nairobi to Maasai Mara

After Safari briefing travel towards Kenyan fertile highlands on the roads built by Italians in the world war two. Keeping an eye for acres of wheat and barley and getting to the Maasai Mara just in time for lunch and an afternoon game drive. Maasai Mara National Reserve is an area of preserved savannah wilderness in southwestern Kenya, along the Tanzanian border. Its animals include lions, cheetahs, elephants, zebras and hippos. Wildebeest traverse its plains during their annual migration. The landscape has grassy plains and rolling hills, and is crossed by the Mara and Talek rivers. The area nearby is dotted with villages (enkangs) of Maasai people.

Day 2: Spend day at Maasai Mara.

After an early breakfast, depart on a morning game drive, returning to the camp mid- morning. After lunch, and maybe relaxation, swim or siesta commence for an afternoon game drive. NB: Stay at Maasai Mara can be extended on visitors request.

Day 3: Maasai Mara to Lake Nakuru

From the Maasai Mara now travel across the Rift Valley, keeping an eye out for herds of giraffe and gazelle as you head up the Mai -Mahiu, before arriving at Lake Nakuru a midday. Once in the lodge after lunch a siesta or a swim, there is an afternoon game drive returning to the lodge as the sun sets. At the park you can enjoy the wide ecological diversity and varied habitats that range from Lake Nakuru itself to the surrounding escarpment and picturesque ridges. Lake Nakuru National Park is ideal for bird watching, hiking, picnic and game drives.

Day 4: Lake Nakuru to Nairobi

Following an early breakfast proceed to Lake Naivasha for a boat ride hence proceed to Nairobi and onwards transfer to the airport or a hotel for ongoing safari arrangements.
