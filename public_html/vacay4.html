Samburu Classic

Day 1: Nairobi to Samburu Reserve

After Safari briefing commence the drive to Samburu national park where you will experience up-close and personal African experience. Once in the lodge after lunch a siesta or a swim , there is an afternoon game drive returning to the lodge as the sun sets. The Samburu National Reserve is a game reserve on the banks of the Ewaso Ng'iro river in Kenya. On the other side of the river is the Buffalo Springs National Reserve. There is a wide variety of animal and bird life seen at Samburu National Reserve. Several large game species common to Kenya's northern plains can be found in abundance here, including the following dry-country fauna: gerenuk, Grevy's zebra, oryx and reticulated giraffe. All three big cats known as the East African lion, Tanzanian cheetah and leopard can also be found here, as well as the elephant, Cape buffalo and Hippopotamus.

Day 2: Spend day at Samburu National Reserve

After an early game drive and breakfast proceed to Nanyuki a market town in Laikipia County on the North West of Mt.Kenya along the A2 road and at the terminus of the branch railway from Nairobi Once in the lodge after lunch a siesta or a swim, there is an afternoon game drive at Ol Pajeta Concervancy returning to the lodge as the sun sets. The Ol Pajeta Conservancy boasts the largest black rhino sanctuary in East Africa, and in 2013 reached a population milestone of 100 black rhino. It also houses the three remaining northern white rhino in the world, who were moved here from Dvur Kr�lov� Zoo in the Czech Republic. The Sweetwaters Chimpanzee Sanctuary is situated here, and provides a haven for orphaned, abandoned and rescued chimpanzees. It is the only place in Kenya where these great apes can be seen. The Conservancy is host to the "Big five game" among a large selection of other African animals, which makes it a popular safari destination.

Day 3: Nanyuki to Lake Nakuru

Following an early breakfast head towards Lake Nakuru. Once in the lodge after lunch a siesta or a swim, there is an afternoon game drive returning to the lodge as the sun sets. At the park you can enjoy the wide ecological diversity and varied habitats that range from Lake Nakuru itself to the surrounding escarpment and picturesque ridges. Lake Nakuru National Park is ideal for bird watching, hiking, picnic and game drives.

Day 5: Lake Nakuru to Maasai Mara

Following an early breakfast proceed to Lake Naivasha for a boat ride hence proceed to Maasai Mara just in time for lunch. After lunch set off on your first game drive, returning to the lodge as the sun sets.

Day 6: Spend day at Maasai Mara

After an early breakfast, depart on a morning game drive, returning to the camp mid- morning. After lunch, and maybe relaxation, swim or siesta commence for an afternoon game drive.

Day 7: Maasai Mara to Nairobi

After an early game drive and breakfast drive out to Nairobi and onwards transfer to the airport or a hotel for ongoing safari arrangements.
