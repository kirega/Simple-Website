Mombasa Classic 2

Day 1: Mombasa to Tsavo east

Pick up from respective hotels (North Coast or South Coast) or airport and drive across the plains of Tsavo and the rolling Taita Hills on the horizon. Once in the lodge after lunch a siesta or a swim, there is an afternoon game drive returning to the lodge as the sun sets. At Tsavo East National Park the sight of dusty red elephant wallowing, rolling and spraying each other with the midnight blue waters of palm-shaded Galana River is one of the most evocative images in Africa. This, along with the 300 kilomtere long Yatta Plateau, the longest lava flow in the world, make for an adventure unlike any other in the Tsavo East. The park forms the largest protected area in Kenya and is home to most of the larger mammals, vast herds of dust red elephant, Rhino, buffalo, lion, leopard, pods of hippo, crocodile, waterbucks, lesser Kudu, gerenuk and the prolific bird life features 500 recorded species.

Day 2: Tsavo east to Amboseli

Following an early breakfast proceed to Amboseli keeping an eye out for Hunters Lodge the sight of the infamous Man eaters of Tsavo where lions attached workers of the railway line. Right before Emali town you will head south towards Amboseli National Park arriving at the park in time for lunch. After lunch a siesta or a swim, there is an afternoon game drive returning to the lodge as the sun sets.

Day 3: Spend the day at Amboseli

Following an early breakfast, depart on a morning game drive, returning to the camp mid- morning. After lunch, and maybe relaxation, swim or siesta commence for an afternoon game drive. Crowned by Mount Kilimanjaro, Africa's highest peak, the Amboseli National Parks is one of Kenya's most popular parks. The name "Amboseli" comes from a Maasai word meaning "salty dust", and it is one of the best places in Africa to view large herds of elephants up close. Nature lovers can explore five different habitats here ranging from the dried-up bed of Lake Amboseli, wetlands with sulphur springs, the savannah and woodlands. They can also visit the local Maasai community who live around the park and experience their authentic culture. Amboseli is best known for its great herds of elephant. You will also spot cheetah, buffalo, giraffe, many types of gazelle and other plains game and it's all set against the amazing backdrop of majestic Mount Kilimanjaro.

Day 4: Amboseli to Lake Nakuru

Following an early game drive and breakfast, continue to Nairobi for an early lunch and to Lake Nakuru for an afternoon game drive returning to the lodge as the sun sets. At the park you can enjoy the wide ecological diversity and varied habitats that range from Lake Nakuru itself to the surrounding escarpment and picturesque ridges. Lake Nakuru National Park is ideal for bird watching, hiking, picnic and game drives.

Day 5: Lake Nakuru to Maasai Mara

Following an early breakfast proceed to Lake Naivasha for a boat ride hence proceed to Maasai Mara just in time for lunch. After lunch set off on your first game drive, returning to the lodge as the sun sets.

Day 6: Spend day at Maasai Mara

After an early breakfast, depart on a morning game drive, returning to the camp mid- morning. After lunch, and maybe relaxation, swim or siesta commence for an afternoon game drive.

Day 7: Maasai Mara to Nairobi

After an early game drive and breakfast drive out to Nairobi and onwards transfer to the airport or a hotel for ongoing safari arrangements.
